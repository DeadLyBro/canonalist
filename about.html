It's time to move beyond Photoshop.

The manga industry has moved online over the last 10 years, but scanlation tech hasn't kept up. Photoshop remains the standard and is quite powerful, but it's also 1) pricey, 2) complicated, and 3) not scanlation-specific. scanlate.io is an attempt to rethink what the process could look like: online, AI-assisted tools, simple enough for anyone to pick up.

It's still very early, but we've got:

  • AI-based Japanese detection & translation
  • Bubble dragging and resizing
  • Typesetting controls
  • Export to .png

What's next:

  • More fonts
  • Redrawing tools
  • Multi-page upload?
  • Collaboration?
  • Your ideas here!

How do I start?

If have no previous experience with scanlating, that's fine! We all started somewhere =) Here's an intro to the main roles involved:

  • Translating -- rewriting the script for a different audience
  • Redrawing -- editing away the Japanese characters
  • Typesetting -- artfully filling in English text
  • Quality checking -- identifying mistakes in any of the above
